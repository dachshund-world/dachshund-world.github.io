Publishing Standards

Dachshund World's publishing standards are shaped by a specific responsibility: this breed's spinal vulnerability means that incorrect or misleading care information can have direct consequences for a dog's health. These standards reflect that responsibility.

IVDD as the Editorial Lens

Every guide on Dachshund World is written with the dachshund's back in mind. Harness recommendations are made because they reduce cervical and spinal strain — that context is always included. Stair access guides exist because jumping is a risk factor for disc events — that is stated, not implied. Weight management guides connect excess weight to increased disc pressure — because that connection is real and matters.

The Dachshund World Content Editors check each guide specifically for whether it treats the breed's spinal vulnerability with the prominence it deserves.

Research Sources

Dachshund World draws on published veterinary literature on chondrodystrophic breeds, IVDD research, and established guidance from dachshund breed organisations. For statistics — particularly IVDD incidence rates — sources are verified before inclusion, because numbers cited without context can be both wrong and misleading.

AI drafting tools are used in the content production process. All AI-drafted content undergoes review and editing by our editorial panel before publication. Content covering IVDD, spinal signs, and emergency situations receives the most careful human review, because errors in these areas carry the highest stakes.

What We Refuse to Do

We will not minimise IVDD risk to make the breed sound less challenging for prospective owners. We will not recommend collars over harnesses to preserve product affiliate relationships. We will not describe stair access as harmless when evidence suggests otherwise. Responsible ownership is better served by accurate information than by reassuring information.

Corrections Policy

Errors are corrected promptly. Where a correction changes advice on a health-relevant topic, the article is updated and an amendment note is added. Readers who identify errors through the Contact page are thanked, and their correction is reviewed within days, not weeks.

Commercial Independence

Affiliate links fund this site. The relationship between affiliate income and editorial content is explained on our funding disclosure page. In short: the editorial panel makes all content decisions independently. A harness that is safer for dachshunds gets recommended even if the commission rate is lower than alternatives.
