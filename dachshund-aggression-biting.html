Why Do Dachshunds Bite? How to Stop Aggression

Understanding Dachshund Aggression

Dachshund aggression is one of the most common behavior problems in the breed. Studies show that 25% of dachshunds display some form of aggression, making it more prevalent than separation anxiety or housetraining issues.

Breed-Specific Barking Traits

  • Hunting heritage: Bred to bark at prey and threats
  • High alert threshold: Small size makes them hypervigilant
  • Pack animal instinct: Barking signals pack members
  • Territorial drive: Protect home/family intensely
  • Attention-seeking: Learned that barking gets response

Root Causes of Dachshund Biting

Cause #1: Fear and Anxiety (40% of aggressive dachshunds)

Fear-based aggression is the most common type in dachshunds. When dachshunds feel threatened, they bite defensively.

Cause #2: Resource Guarding (30% of aggressive dachshunds)

Resource guarding is intense protection of food, toys, or territory.

Treatment Solutions

Most dachshund aggression responds well to training and desensitization protocols.
